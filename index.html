Ahoy!

Welcome to my little website. Glad you decided to stop by.

I’ve had my hands in a lot of different pies over the years—digital magazine editing, indie bookselling, and philanthropy among them—but nowadays, I mostly split my time between being a candidate for an MFA in Fiction at the Bennington Writing Seminars and freelance writing and editing. When I’m not reading and writing like a motherfucker (as Cheryl Strayed so wisely advises), I’m focused on Midnight Breakfast, a literary magazine I co-founded and became Editor-in-Chief of in 2014. Just last year, The Billfold interviewed us about the inner-workings of that process, how we build community through readership, and what it means to pay folks for their work in this day and age.

Forever voracious, I’m always on the lookout for eclectic and challenging new projects and collaborators, so if what you see below grabs you, please feel free to drop me a line. Though I primarily lean towards making words work and literary assignments, helping people effectively communicate their ideas through mindful content strategy is also an interest.

I currently live in San Francisco, and spend my off-hours cooking (and ogling food porn on Instagram), drinking copious amounts of tea, and playing far too much Scrabble, Two Dots, and 1010!. I also have an M. Phil. in Film History and Theory from Trinity College Dublin, which I put to good use by winning Oscar betting pools each year.
